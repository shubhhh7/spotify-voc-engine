# 🎨 Presentation Template Guide

## 10-Slide Deck Structure for Fellowship Submission

### Slide 1: Title
- **Title:** "Spotify Music Discovery: A Voice of Customer Analysis"
- **Subtitle:** "Analyzing 4,000+ user feedback entries across Reddit, App Store, and Play Store"
- **Your name, date, fellowship name**
- **Visual:** Spotify logo + clean background

### Slide 2: The Problem (1 powerful insight)
- **Headline:** "Users aren't discovering music — they're trapped in algorithmic loops"
- **1 powerful quote:** "[Exact quote from top_evidence.csv]"
- **1 stat:** "X% of analyzed feedback mentions discovery frustration"
- **Visual:** Large quote text, minimal design

### Slide 3: Methodology (credibility)
- **Headline:** "How we listened at scale"
- **3 data sources with volumes:**
  - Reddit: X posts + Y comments
  - App Store: X reviews
  - Play Store: X reviews
- **1 sentence on AI + human validation:** "AI-assisted extraction, human red-teaming"
- **1 sentence on limitations:** "English-only, snapshot, selection bias acknowledged"
- **Visual:** Simple flow diagram (sources → cleaning → AI → validation → insights)

### Slide 4: The Key Insight (contrarian finding)
- **Headline:** "The real problem isn't bad recommendations — it's recommendation fatigue"
- **3 bullets explaining the insight:**
  - Users are overwhelmed by too many algorithmic options
  - They want curation, not more optimization
  - This creates a paradox: better algorithm = more fatigue
- **Evidence:** 1 quote + 1 number
- **Visual:** Split screen — "What we assumed" vs "What users actually want"

### Slide 5: User Segments (3 personas)
- **Headline:** "3 distinct discovery behaviors"
- **3 columns:**

| The Playlist Prisoner | The Musical Explorer | The Background Listener |
|----------------------|---------------------|------------------------|
| "Stuck in Daily Mix loops" | "Actively seeking novelty" | "Wants zero-friction" |
| 35% of pain points | 28% of pain points | 22% of pain points |
| "[Quote]" | "[Quote]" | "[Quote]" |
| Wants: curation | Wants: depth | Wants: simplicity |

- **Visual:** 3 cards with icons, names, and quotes

### Slide 6: The Data (4 charts)
- **Headline:** "What the data shows"
- **Chart 1:** Pain category distribution (bar chart)
- **Chart 2:** Sentiment by source (stacked bar — Reddit vs App Store vs Play Store)
- **Chart 3:** Rating distribution (histogram)
- **Chart 4:** Top unmet needs (horizontal bar)
- **Visual:** 2×2 grid of charts, clean labels

### Slide 7: JTBD Map (the framework)
- **Headline:** "Jobs users hire Spotify to do"
- **Table:**

| When I... | I want to... | So I can... | But Spotify... |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|----------------|
| Commute | Discover new music | Feel energized | Plays the same 50 songs |
| Work | Find focus music | Stay productive | Interrupts with skips |
| Exercise | Match my intensity | Push harder | Can't find the right tempo |

- **Visual:** Journey-style diagram or clean table

### Slide 8: Opportunities (ICE scoring)
- **Headline:** "5 ranked opportunities"
- **Table:**

| # | Opportunity | Impact | Confidence | Ease | ICE | Evidence |
|---|-------------|--------|------------|------|-----|----------|
| 1 | [Name] | 9 | 8 | 6 | 12.0 | [1-sentence] |

### Slide 9: Competitive Context
- **Headline:** "Where Spotify is vulnerable"
- **3 competitive threats:**
  1. Apple Music's human curation → appeals to "Playlist Prisoners"
  2. TikTok's discovery → appeals to "Musical Explorers"
  3. YouTube Music's variety → appeals to "Background Listeners"
- **1 defensible advantage:** "Spotify's data moat is still strong for..."
- **Visual:** Competitive positioning map (2×2 matrix)

### Slide 10: Recommendation
- **Headline:** "3 specific next steps"
- **3 time-bound actions:**
  1. "Validate [insight] with quantitative panel survey (2 weeks)"
  2. "Prototype [feature] for Playlist Prisoner segment (4 weeks)"
  3. "A/B test [change] against control (6 weeks)"
- **1 risk statement:** "If we don't act: [specific consequence]"
- **Visual:** Timeline or 3 numbered cards

## 🎨 Design Tips

**Color palette:**
- Primary: Spotify Green (#1DB954)
- Secondary: Dark Gray (#191414)
- Accent: White (#FFFFFF)
- Highlight: Light Green (#1ED760)

**Typography:**
- Headlines: Bold, 32-40pt
- Body: Regular, 18-24pt
- Quotes: Italic, 24pt, in a colored box
- Numbers: Bold, 48pt+ for key stats

**Rules:**
- One insight per slide
- Every claim has a quote or number
- No walls of text (max 6 bullets per slide)
- Use real quotes from users — authenticity wins
- Source citations on every quote ("Reddit, 245 upvotes")

**Tools:**
- Google Slides (free, collaborative)
- Figma (free tier, better design control)
- Canva (free tier, templates)

**Export:**
- PDF backup (in case presentation software fails)
- Speaker notes for each slide (2-3 sentences)
